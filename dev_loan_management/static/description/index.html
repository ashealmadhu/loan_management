Loan Management System in Odoo for Customer and Supplier

Loan Management System in Odoo is a complete solution for efficiently managing loans for customers and suppliers. It supports streamlined processing of loan applications, approvals, disbursements, and repayments, ensuring a smooth workflow for financial operations. The app allows the creation of customizable loan categories, borrower profiles, and eligibility criteria, enabling businesses to cater to diverse needs. Users can upload and validate loan verification documents, generate loan-specific accounts, and set installment reminders for timely repayments.

This system integrates seamlessly with Odoo's accounting module, offering real-time financial insights, including detailed journal entries for each loan installment. Managers can approve loan requests, disburse funds, and monitor repayment statuses using structured workflows that include states like draft, confirm, approve, disburse, open, and close. Automated notifications keep managers and borrowers informed about upcoming installments and overdue payments.

The app also provides robust reporting features, such as installment summaries, loan account summaries, and loan collection reports, available in both PDF and on-screen formats. Borrowers can access loan details, notices, agreements, and outstanding reports through the portal, which includes digital signature capabilities for agreements and notices.

With features like interest certificate generation, loan agreement and notice templates, processing fee invoicing, and borrower-specific loan summaries, the system is a comprehensive tool for managing loan operations effectively. Whether it’s a home loan inquiry or a business loan application, the app ensures accurate documentation, seamless communication, and efficient loan lifecycle management.

The app includes an advanced dashboard designed for loan analysis, empowering users with data-driven decision-making capabilities. The dashboard allows users to refine data using filters such as user, customer, loan type, and date range, ensuring precise insights tailored to specific needs. It provides comprehensive statistics, including key metrics like approved loan amounts, disbursed loan amounts, repayment totals, interest amounts, new leads, processing fees, closed loans, and average interest rates. For visual representation, the dashboard features charts showcasing trends such as loan requests over the last three months, principal versus interest analysis, paid versus unpaid installments, stage-wise loan status overview, loan volume categorized by loan type, top partners based on loan amounts, and installment statuses. Additionally, the dashboard includes data tables for detailed views of upcoming installments and overdue payments, making it a powerful tool for monitoring and optimizing loan operations effectively.

Loan Management Dashboard

Flexible Loan & Installment Scheduling

Provides an option to select the loan duration by week, month, or year.

Provides an option to select installment duration by day, week, month, quarter, semiannual, or annual period.

Installments are created according to loan duration, installment duration, and start date.

For a loan with a duration of 1 week, an installment frequency of 1 day, and a start date of September 1, a total of 7 installments are created, starting from September 1.

Grace Period on Loan Repayments

Configure a grace period for loan type's on principal or principal + interest.

When creating a loan request, the grace period is applied based on the selected loan type.

If the grace period is applied to the principal amount, the borrower is not required to pay the principal during the grace period and only pays the interest. After the grace period ends, both principal and interest must be paid.

If the grace period is applied to both principal and interest, the borrower is not required to make any payments during the grace period. After the grace period ends, both principal and interest must be paid.

Penalty Calculation with Grace Period

Configure penalty rate , penalty grace days , penalty product in loan type

When creating a loan request, the penalty rate and grace days for penalty are applied.

Based on the grace days and penalty rate, the penalty amount is calculated, and the borrower must pay the penalty first.

If the installment date is August 26 and the current date is August 29, with a penalty grace period of 2 days, the borrower must pay the penalty amount. The penalty is calculated based on the penalty rate, principal amount, and the number of days past the grace period.

If the borrower tries to pay an installment before paying the penalty, a warning is displayed.

If the borrower attempts to pay a later installment before clearing previous unpaid installments, a warning is displayed.

After paying the penalty, the user can view the penalty invoice and payment details.

Postpone Installments

Borrowers have the option to postpone their loan installments.

Installments can be postponed by a specified number of days, weeks, or months, depending on the installment duration.

If an installment scheduled for the 1st of every month is postponed by one day, the new installment date is updated to the 2nd of every month in the installment schedule.

After postponing an installment, the changes can be viewed in a separate tab.

For example, if an installment was scheduled for September 1 and is postponed by one day, the new date of September 2 will apply to all subsequent installments in the schedule.
